RWI runs two liquidity pools. The main pool pairs $RWI with $WETH — trading fees there fund the donations. The secondary pool pairs $RWI with $PONS: we call it the earn-and-burn pool, because everything the project earns from it is sent straight to the burn address. Pons already routes its platform fees into $PONS buybacks; we take it a step further and destroy our share outright, permanently shrinking the supply of the token behind the launchpad that gave $RWI its start. Rising tide, fewer tokens.

03 — The mechanism

Three moves, repeated forever.

Trade

Every swap of $RWI on Robinhood Chain generates creator revenue automatically. No taxes on holders, no special mechanics — just volume doing work.

Donate

Revenue is claimed and sent to verified charitable organizations. Recipients are named before funds move, never after.

Prove

Every donation is published with its receipt and transaction hash. If it isn't on the ledger, it didn't happen.

04 — The premise

Most coins never leave the chain. This one does.

Crypto is very good at moving value around inside itself. $RWI points that machinery outward: creator revenue generated by trading is converted into real donations to vetted charities — food banks, children's hospitals, hunger relief. Not someday. On a schedule, with proof.

05 — The chain

Built on Robinhood Chain.

$RWI lives on Robinhood Chain (chain ID 4663) — fast, cheap, and close to where the next hundred million people will touch crypto for the first time. A serious mission deserves rails people can actually reach. $RWI launched fixed-supply on Pons, the chain’s leading launchpad — no mint function, no surprises.
