00 — Abstract

The one-line thesis.

Real World Impact ($RWI) is a memecoin with a single, verifiable job: convert the natural fee revenue of a traded token into published donations to vetted charities. There is no vesting cliff, no roadmap of future features to unlock, and no discretionary treasury. What happens onchain is the entire product, and the product is proof.

This document describes the mechanism, the tokenomics, the burn flywheel that ties $RWI back to the launchpad it was born on (Pons), and the disclosure standard the project holds itself to.

01 — The premise

Crypto points inward. RWI points out.

Crypto is very good at moving value around inside itself. Tokens buy tokens, protocols reward protocols, and the loop closes. That is fine — closed loops are how liquidity forms — but they are not, on their own, a claim on the world. $RWI is an attempt to route a fraction of that internal motion outward on a fixed schedule, without turning the effort into a charity that happens to have a token, or a token that happens to gesture at charity. The mechanism is the marketing. The receipts are the pitch.

Nothing about $RWI is investment advice, and nothing here promises price behavior. What it does promise is procedural: fees flow, donations are made, and each one is published with the transaction hash that settled it. If it is not on the ledger, it did not happen.

02 — How it works

Fees in, receipts out.

$RWI runs two liquidity pools on Uniswap: a main $RWI/$WETH pool that funds the donations, and a smaller $RWI/$PONS pool whose entire fee stream is burned. Section 03 covers the burn side. This section covers the donations.

Every buy and sell in the main pool pays a trading fee back into the pool. As the fee-earning creator of the token, the project's share of those fees accrues in a wallet on a rolling basis. When it's time to donate, that balance is sent to a vetted charity, and the transaction hash is published alongside the announcement.

01Trading in the main $RWI/$WETH pool generates fee revenue
02Fees accrue to the RWI wallet in $ETH
03$ETH is donated to a vetted charity — receipt published

Recipients are not chosen by governance vote or on a rotation. Donations flow through The Giving Block, a crypto-native donation platform that pre-vets every nonprofit in its network against nationally recognized standards — 990 filings, Charity Navigator ratings, and organizational transparency. The recipient list to date spans hunger relief, homelessness, children's health, and children's hospitals. The vetting layer means the trust burden sits with an accredited third-party service, not the project alone.

03 — The burn

Extending the Pons flywheel.

$RWI was launched on Pons, a launchpad on Robinhood Chain whose economic model routes its platform fees into $PONS buybacks. Every project that succeeds on Pons therefore feeds back into the token that made the launch possible in the first place. RWI takes that flywheel one step further with a deliberate two-pool architecture.

The main liquidity pool pairs $RWI with $WETH. Trading fees earned there are the source of the donations described in the next section — the working capital of the mission. Alongside it sits a second, dedicated pool that pairs $RWI with $PONS. We call it the earn-and-burn pool: the project's entire share of $PONS accrued here is sent directly to a burn address. Never sold, never spent, permanently removed from circulation. The burn wallet is public: 0x9CD7C9196A4C1836A3DF089cb210272e07e6A5e5. Its balance can only grow, and it can be watched in real time on the RWI site.

The reasoning is straightforward. A stronger Pons means a healthier launchpad, which means a healthier Robinhood Chain, which means a bigger stage for the mission that $RWI exists to fund. Burning our share of $PONS is the most direct way to contribute to that outcome that does not involve selling into the market we depend on. Rising tide, fewer tokens.

04 — The ledger

Proof, not promises.

The word "transparent" is close to meaningless in crypto — everything is transparent, and yet very little is legible. RWI's disclosure standard is a small commitment with real teeth: every donation is announced publicly, both on X and on the site's ledger, with two links attached to it. The first is the onchain settlement receipt. The second is the public announcement post. Either can be independently verified without trusting the project.

05 — Token & contract

What exists, what does not.

Deployment

$RWI was deployed on Robinhood Chain (chain ID 4663) on July 18, 2026. The token contract is 0x2286397228be256529BE1ae9ed8D7d16549e9C6A. The main liquidity pool pairs $RWI with $WETH on Uniswap at 0xff6aa24815d1274a9be0cfd17c7c7489cd40a697. A second, dedicated $RWI/$PONS pool at 0x62a9e7cf94049dded854c26ff6204079a1c8caed operates as the earn-and-burn pool described in section 03 — separate from the main venue and designed so that everything the project earns from it is destroyed.

Supply

Total supply is 1,000,000,000 $RWI, fixed at deployment. There is no mint function, and no owner privileges to change balances or freeze accounts. What was deployed is what exists, and it will not be changed. Any tokens sent to a burn address are removed from circulation permanently and can be counted onchain against total supply.

What RWI is not

$RWI is not a security, is not a claim on a treasury, and is not a governance token. Holders do not vote on donation targets and are not entitled to any share of fee revenue directly. The project's charitable output is committed procedurally — through published donations against a public ledger — rather than contractually. This is a design choice: procedural commitments can be verified in the moment, while contractual ones require legal enforcement no memecoin can credibly provide.

Fee revenue

Trading fees accrue to the project wallet on an ongoing basis. Fees earned in the main $RWI/$WETH pool are converted to $ETH and donated. Fees earned in the earn-and-burn $RWI/$PONS pool are burned in full. No portion of either stream is retained as personal compensation to the founder, and there is no development treasury being carved out for future spending.

06 — What changes, what doesn't

The load-bearing parts stay.

The site will evolve, the receipts list will grow, and the front-end tooling around the project will keep improving. What will not change is the mechanism at the center: fee revenue is either donated to a vetted charity or burned. That is the whole product.

Because the mechanism is procedural, so are its failure states. RWI has stopped being RWI if any of the following occurs — and each is observable directly, by anyone, without permission.

01The main pool's fee revenue is directed anywhere other than donations
02$PONS from the earn-and-burn pool is sold instead of sent to the burn wallet
03Donations are made silently — no announcement, no tx hash
04Announced donations don't settle onchain, or settle outside The Giving Block's vetted network
05The founder retains fee revenue as personal income

None of these require a press release to detect. They only require someone to check.

08 — Risks & disclaimers

Read this part carefully.

$RWI is a memecoin. It is a highly volatile digital asset with no earnings, no cash flows accruing to holders, and no intrinsic value floor. It is entirely possible for it to trade to zero, and holders should not purchase it with capital they are unwilling to lose entirely.

Nothing in this document is investment, tax, or legal advice. The charitable activity described here is a feature of how project revenue is deployed; it does not make $RWI a security, a donation-tax vehicle, or a substitute for direct giving. Purchasers do not receive a tax deduction for buying the token, and the project does not act as a fiscal agent or intermediary for donors seeking one — donations are made from project revenue after it accrues.

Robinhood Chain is a newer network and carries the usual smart-contract, bridge, and liquidity risks that come with any early-stage chain. Users should conduct their own diligence, verify contract addresses directly rather than trusting linked material, and interact only through wallets they control.
